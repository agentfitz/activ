1. Preamble

Before starting, there's a few informations you need to know, just to make everything easier.

Pages Settings

Every setting regarding a page (or a showcase) is located inside the edit page mode of WordPress itself, not in the Options Panel. That means that every page has its own little option panel.

Thumbnails (Images Resizing)

Unlike the majority of the WordPress Themes, Daydream does not use the popular image resizing script TimThumb. The reason why I decided to drop it is pretty simple: I didn't want to rely on any external script that may compromise my users's security and I wanted to rely completely on the WordPress engine.

The difference between the two methods of resizing is that TimThumb can recreate every image on-the-fly, while WordPress creates a series of copies of your image of different sizes after you upload them. So if you change, for instance, the size of the blog featured images on the theme Options Panel, from 100 pixels to 200 pixels, WordPress will not find the 200 pixels images and show the 100 pixels one, simply because when you uploaded them the image size was configured to 100.

However, even if it sounds a little bit problematic, there's an easy solution. Using a WordPress plugin you can recreate every Thumbnail at any time. I've included a copy of the best Thumbnail Rebuilder around at the moment ("Ajax Thumbnail Rebuild"). Simply install it and remember to refresh your images after you change some image parameter setting.

2. Getting Started

1. Theme Installation

Installing a WordPress theme is the most easy thing in the world! There are two ways to install it:

"I know what FTP is": Using your favorite FTP application, upload the "Daydream" folder in your server's /wp-content/themes folder.

Via WordPress: Go to Appearance → Install Themes → Upload and choose the "Daydream.zip" file. Click "Install Now" to install the theme.

Once you have uploaded it, go to Appearance → Themes and finally activate it!

2. Getting Started

2. Configuring the Main Menu

This theme makes use of the WordPress Nav Menu feature, so you can configure your Main Menu simply dragging and dropping your elements.

To configure the Main Menu from scratch, go to Appearance → Menu, click on the + Icon and create it.

Now on the left side of the Menu Manager, select your Menu under the Theme Locations block and save. That's it! Now you can start filling your menu with your items.

Tip: To create a Drop-down menu, drag a menu item slightly on the right of his parent.

2. Getting Started

3. Configuring the Footer

Even if it is positioned at the end of every page, the Footer can be considered as one of the most important part of a website. It may contain legal informations or useful or links to the page's author or to a social profile like Twitter or Facebook.

The footer of this theme consists in two blocks. The left block is a content block that you can fill with anything you want, while the right one contains links to your social network profiles.

To configure the content of the left block, go to the Appearance → Daydream and select the General tab. Now scroll to Footer Info and fill the Left Area textarea with anything you want. You can also use the theme's Shortcodes to add some cool stuff!

2. Getting Started

3. Setting up the Homepage

To configure your homepage, create a page (e.g. "Homepage"), and assign to it the "Widgetized Homepage" Page Template.

To set the page you just created as "Front page", go to Settings → Reading. Now switch the "Front page displays" input to "A static page" and select your page under the "Front page:" select box. Don't forget to hit the "Save Changes" button.

The Content

There are two ways to configure your Homepage content. You can simply enter your content inside your page when in the edit mode, or you can use Widgets. After assigning the Page Template to your page, you will notice that 3 new sidebar areas magically appeared in the Appearance → Widgets section of WordPress.

Every Widget that you'll add in these Sidebars will be displayed in your Homepage. Have a look at the available Widgets and see what you can achieve with them.

2. Getting Started

4. Configuring the Slider

This theme includes 3 powerful Homepage Sliders:

  1. Daydream Slider
  2. Nivo Slider by Dev7studios
  3. Orbit Slider by ZURB

To select your favorite Slider, open the Daydream Options Panel located in Appearance → Daydream. Now switch to the "Slider Area" tab and choose a slider. Hit the "Save Settings" button to save your settings (no way!)

You can manage the Homepage Slides in the "Slides" tab.

The Daydream Slider

While the "Nivo Slider" and the "Orbit Slider" are image-based Sliders (and do not require any explanation) the Daydream Slider offers a much more bigger range of options and layout combinations. Let's see how it works.

Responsive Layout

The Daydream Slider configures your Slides depending on what fields you compile. For instance, if you setup a Slide without assigning a Picture to it, the Slide will be wider and fill the whole horizontal space.

Otherwise, if you use both Picture and Content, a "Content Layout" option will appear in the Slides Panel and the Slide will change as well.

The same concept applies to the "Lightbox" option, that is displayed only if you configure a "Url" option.

More Than Pictures

The Daydream Slider also gives you the choice to use a Video as Picture. The only thing to do is enter a Video Url (Youtube, Vimeo or a HTML5 Video) inside the "Picture" input.

If you don't need a Slider, you can choose the "None" option from the Sliders List or enter a custom content in the Slider Area tab of the Daydream Options Panel.

You can display a Slider in other pages as well, using the [slider] Shortcode.

5. Setting up the Blog

To configure a Blog all you have to do is create a page (e.g. "Blog"), go to Settings → Reading, switch the "Front page displays" input to "A static page" and select your page under the "Posts page:" select box. Don't forget to hit the "Save Changes" button.

That's it! You can find all the available options for the Blog in the "Layout" tab of the Daydream Options Panel.

7. Setting up a Showcase

Daydream gives you the possibility to create infinite Showcase Galleries with individual settings.

Creating a Showcase is amazingly simple. All you have to do is create a new page (e.g. Showcase), assign to that page the "Daydream Showcase" Page Template and hit the blue "Update" button.

After you do that, a new Post Type with the same name of your page will magically appear on the left side of the WordPress Administration.

More importantly, your page will be transformed from a normal page to a Showcase Page, so a little Option Panel will take the place of the editor.

3. Showcase

1. Adding Items

Adding items to a Showcase is just like adding Posts to a Blog. All you have to do is "open" your Showcase Post Type (named as your Showcase page) and hit the "Add New Item" link.

Now, depending on the "Gallery Layout" option you have selected on your Showcase page, a series of option will be available. For instance, if you selected the "Slideshow" option, no editor will be visualized because Slideshow Items do not need any content.

A Showcase Item consists of a Featured Image, a Title, a Excerpt (a short description) and the Content. What you will insert inside the Content block will be displayed as a page when the item is clicked.

Featured Images

The Featured Image is basically the picture that will be displayed in the Showcase to represent your item. To apply it, simply click the "Set Featured Image" link located under the "Featured Image" block. A Lightbox will appear, now choose your image, select a size (usually "Full Size") and click the "Use as featured image" link.

3. Showcase

2. Assigning Filters

This theme gives you the chance to assign multiple Tags or "Filters" to your Showcase Items so that your users can navigate your Gallery or Portfolio and choose to filter your items basing on the category you have assigned to them (e.g. Logos, Interfaces, Prints).

To assign a Filter to your Showcase Item, visualize it in the edit mode, and start adding them as you would do with Tags on normal WordPress Posts.

Fin! Now your Showcase Page will display all the available items in the "Tools" are of the Website's Layout.

3. Showcase

3. Using the Lightbox

If you want to show a Lightbox when clicking to your Showcase Item, open it in the edit mode and check the "Use Lightbox" checkbox. Always remember to click the blue "Update" button to apply your changes.

After you do that, a series of options will appear.

Image Lightbox

  • Url: The url to the picture you want to show inside the Lightbox. By default it is the Featured Image.
  • Caption: The caption to show in the open Lightbox. By default the excerpt will be used as Caption.
  • Gallery: You have also the option to group various Gallery item. To do that, use the same "Gallery" value on the items you want to group together.

Video Lightbox

To show a video inside your Lightbox, simply enter the url to your Youtube, Vimeo or HTML5 Video.

Webpage Lightbox

You can also show any web page inside the Lightbox. To do that, simply enter the url inside the "Url" field. By default the Showcase Item page will be displayed.

This Text Widget differs from the WordPress one for the possibility to use Shortcodes inside of it.

Video

With this Widget you can add any Youtube, Vimeo or HTML5 Video inside your page.

What's a HTML5 Video?

The fresh HTML5 introduced a new way to add videos to a website, using a more direct HTML tag made just for that, avoiding any external plugin like Flash and pushing the internet to the future!

This theme uses the VideoJS jQuery Plugin to give them a nice UI, better controls and a Flash fallback for browsers that still do not support the HTML5 <video> tag.

The supported Video formats for HTML5 Videos are .mp4, .ogv and .webm. This theme will automatically search for all video formats in the same folder of the provided video.

Screenshots

This Widget, initially conceived for the Homepage, let you add three columns of big links associated with great icons with the main purpose of giving emphasis to your product or website's feature. It gives you the possibility to use any link and also activate a Lightbox.

Testimonials

This Widget displays a cool Slider that will automatically display the posts from the Category you've chosen as Slides containing a picture of your testimonial (Featured Image), the message (the Content of the post) and his name (the Title).

6. Extras & Tips

Daydream includes a few Extra Options that will make the whole experience of using the theme a lot easier. You can find all Extras in the Options Panel, under the "Extras" tab. Appearance → Daydream → Extras.

Categories Exclusion

These theme gives you the possibility to exclude any Post Category you want from all user-accessible areas of your website. This functions come in very handy, for instance, when using the "Testimonials Slider" Widget or Shortcode. So you can add your "Testimonials" posts hiding them from the Blog or any other area.

Theme Updates

If you like to keep your themes up-to-date, you can choice to enable the Updates Notification, so when a new version of Daydream will be available you'll be notified.

Functions Tips

If you find the Column Shortcodes too difficult or boring to use, here's a tip for you: create a custom Sidebar in the Daydream Options Panel (Appearance → Daydream → Sidebars), build your columns using the Columns Widget, then call your sidebar in your page using the Sidebar Shortcode.

7. Technical Details

Here's some informations that may help if you want to customize the theme even more.

Colours Folder

Every colour theme is located inside the daydream/colours/ folder. A colour consists in a folder containing a stylesheet named "theme.css" and the related images.

Theme Translation

Daydream is translation-ready. That means that editing the ".po" files included inside the daydream/languages/ folder you can translate the theme in any other language.

When editing a language, do not remove strings like "%s" or "%1$". These are strings that get automatically replaced with dynamic or more complex content.
